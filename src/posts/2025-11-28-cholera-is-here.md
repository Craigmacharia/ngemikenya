Kenya's cholera outbreak, declared in late February 2025, has ravaged six counties including Nairobi, Kisumu, Migori, Kwale, Mombasa, and Turkana, fueled by heavy rains, flooding, and poor sanitation in informal settlements and refugee camps. Over 157,000 cases and 2,148 deaths were recorded continent-wide by May, with Kenya reporting hundreds locally and a 5.2% fatality rate exceeding WHO's 1% threshold. The Ministry of Health, backed by WHO, launched Kenya's first mass vaccination drive targeting 2.2 million people, while rapid teams enhance surveillance, water testing, and rehydration efforts amid climate-driven risks.
